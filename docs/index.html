MOVA

Mean Open Vulnerability Age

MTTR tells you how fast work gets closed. MOVA tells you how old the vulnerability backlog still open is right now.

The core idea is simple: MTTR can rise while exposure falls. When teams finally close older backlog, the age of closed work goes up, so MTTR can look worse even while the backlog gets healthier.

Two different signals

MTTR

Closure speed

How long did the work that got closed take to remediate?

MOVA

Backlog age

How old is the work still open and still carried today?

MTTR measures flow. MOVA measures stock. You need both to understand what is actually improving.

What MOVA reveals

  • Whether older exposure is actually being reduced
  • Whether teams are closing meaningful backlog or just recent work
  • Whether a low MTTR is hiding an aging tail of unresolved findings

How to use it

MOVA does not replace MTTR. It complements it. Report both side by side, along with open count and a backlog-age threshold such as 180+ days open, to understand both remediation speed and remaining exposure.
