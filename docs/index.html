Why Vulnerability MTTR Alone Misleads

Use MTTR and MOVA to read the system.

MTTR and MOVA answer different questions. Read together, they show whether remediation is changing the system or only changing the dashboard.

The deck uses one simulated team, fixed capacity, and two simplified closure patterns to show how the signals diverge.

Core idea

Same data. Different questions.

Flow

MTTR

How old was the work that closed?

Backlog age

MOVA

How old is the work still open today?

MTTR is windowed. Label the window before calling a regression. MOVA is not windowed; it reflects backlog age right now.

Read them together

What the pair reveals

  • Whether closure speed and backlog age are moving in different directions
  • Whether faster-looking closures are leaving older findings behind
  • Whether remediation progress is real or cosmetic

What to report

Keep the dashboard focused

  • MTTR for closure flow
  • MOVA for open backlog age
  • The time window used for MTTR before calling a regression
  • The prioritization context behind the movement

Prioritization

Fix what matters first

Do not prioritize by newest-first or oldest-first alone. Use risk-based inputs, then use MTTR and MOVA to see what those decisions are doing to the system.

  • Severity, such as CVSS
  • Exploitability, such as KEV or weaponized PoC
  • Exposure and reachability
  • Asset criticality
  • Threat intelligence and business context

Takeaway

Read the system, not just the metric.

Use the pair to verify whether risk-based prioritization is producing the outcome you intended.
